COMMUNITY DEVELOPMENT

Water supply continues to be a major problem in rural areas and as such we engage contractors in drilling boreholes in all street quarters of Osi- Ekiti, Ekiti State and neighboring communities like Igbole-Ekiti and Awo-Ekiti. We have commissioned a total of 11 boreholes around 20 villages and communities to compliment their supply of clean drinking water. Side road clearing is also a major part of our contribution to the community , the safety of commuters on our road cannot be overemphasized thus maintaining a good road network was part of our activities this we believe will reduce the rate of auto crash and also reduce the activities of criminal element on the highway due to overgrown shrub on both sides of the road. Mercy International Mission also hopes to partner with local and international organizations such as the International Institute of Tropical Agriculture (IITA) to bring agricultural projects to Osi- Ekiti and surrounding communities . The aim of this project is to provide jobs, food sufficiency and commerce for the local communities.

In addition to our main vision of providing access to basic medical care services, Mercy International Mission is also contributing to the development of the host village, Osi-Ekiti. Via our voluntary community service initiative, the youths in Osi Ekiti are mobilized as the need arises to undertake maintenance work on community utilities. Some of the features of the initiative includes: cutting of grasses in public squares, cleaning of the town hall, doing palliative repair works on community roads among others. We hope to replicate this in all or most of the villages within our coverage zone in the coming years. We also continued to support the women and youth that received small micro loans from Mercy International, helping them to address various challenges facing them. TESTIMONIALS We have several testimonies of what Mercy International meant for the people of Nigeria and what it has done for individuals within the community. To mention just a few, during Aramoko-Ekiti outreach, an old woman suffering from Osteoarthritis to the extent that she was unable to walk was treated and given medication by the mobile clinic team for few weeks. At some point, a call was received from her children that she can now walk without pain. Also, a patient was rushed to the clinic with history of diarrhea for about three days and was very weak and dehydrated. After being assessed he came up with hypotension and was placed on intravenous fluid normal saline and dextrose water with some other drugs. He regained his strength after administering the medications. A 65 years old man with a swollen leg and a deep injury was brought to the clinic on the 21st of August 2018. His leg wound was extremely severe, to the extent that he could not walk without significant assistance. Mercy International Mission staff treated him, beginning with an incision and drainage to the wound site, as it was large and septic. After this, they began a regimen of daily dressing with topical antibiotics and honey. The wound healed after (3) three months of treatment.
