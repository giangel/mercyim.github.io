Health Care Programs

Mercy International Mission launched a Medical mission in February of 2012 to address the deficit in health care delivery in rural southwestern part of Nigeria. We address these challenges using a 3-pronged approach which includes stationary clinics, mobile health vans, and annual large-scale healthcare events. The Mobile clinics operate in Ekiti state and environs, providing healthcare in over 20 towns and communities. The mobile clinic provides basic screening, treatment, and prevention for common infectious diseases.

We are also exploring the role of telemedicine in the provision of basic health care services. At Mercy International Mission, we engage the services of telemedicine companies to provide health services across countries in Africa and the United States. It is our strong belief that the addition of telehealth will help expand the volume of patients reached and provide access to a greater breadth of medical specialties. However, barriers like power and internet access have been challenges to actualizing optimal implementation of this initiative.

Mobile Clinics

Launched on April 16, 2012, Mercy's Mobile Clinic vans run from Monday through Friday, 10 AM - 3 PM. The Van travels from village to village providing medical care and screening to people of Osi-Ekiti and its Environs.

Malaria Prevention

Mercy International Mission distributes mosquito nets to children and expecting mothers. We also have reserves of nets at our clinic facility for those in need as at when needed.
