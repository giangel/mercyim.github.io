Mercy International Medical Clinic

Mercy International Medical Clinic is a full-service clinic designed to serve the rural villages of Southwest Nigeria. The clinic will see 12,000 to 15,000 patients annually, mostly through outpatient services like primary care; infection control; immunizations; pharmacy services; detection, prevention, and treatment of diseases such as HIV/AIDS, malaria, and tuberculosis; and obstetrics and neonatal care. Additional services will include cardiac and vision care and X-rays. A comprehensive patient information system will enable the transfer of patient records to other medical facilities in the region, with all records electronically collected and centrally stored. The clinic will also have an on-site laboratory to conduct and analyze doctor-ordered diagnostic tests. Mercy International Mission, the USA, and Mercy International Mission, Nigeria exist to reduce disease and mortality rates in southwestern Nigeria through the provision of essential medical care, preventative health services, and health education; to strengthen local communities by facilitating self-development activities and to improve overall quality of life for men, women, and children in the region. To accomplish this mission, we realize that we have to establish and maintain a permanent self-sustaining health clinic that can service the entire Ekiti and the neighboring states. We intend to build a solid, efficient, and cost-effective diagnostic medical clinic that can meet the need of the people in this area. The clinic will provide: • General medical and pediatric consultations and minor general surgical procedures • Ophthalmological consultations and dispensing of eyeglasses • Dispensing medications, supplements, and multivitamins to children and adults • Housing the mobile clinic vehicles • Providing mosquito nets to prevent Malaria • Installing and distributing water purification systems
