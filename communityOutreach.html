COMMUNITY OUTREACH AND COUNSELING

The annual medical mission outreach consist of medical services, community outreach and spiritual counseling. Each patient generally go through each of these stages during the outreach. It was great to see the number of people from the local area that were willing to volunteer during the 2018 Medical Mission. In fact, we are glad to see that the community has truly adopted Mercy International as their own. We had several individuals and families that donated yams, chicken, rice, water, etc. toward the medical mission. This act of kindness on the part of the community help reduce our outreach expenses.

STAFF & VOLUNTEERS

Thanks to the hard work and diligence of the staff and volunteer team, the 2018 mission was very successful. In addition to Mercy International staff, there were more than 65 volunteers that made up 2018 Medical Mission Team, consisting of Surgeons, Ophthalmologist (eye doctors), Dentists, Dental Assistants, Medical Doctors, Nurses, Pharmacy Technicians, Pastors, Counselors, Prayer Team, Youth Leaders, and a Crowd Control Team. As the number of volunteers increases, we felt the need to include volunteer training and orientation as part of our medical mission schedule. The mission was supported by medical/general professionals across 7 core patient care units including Health & Wellness Education, Triage/Consultation, Eye Care, Dental Care, Surgery, Pharmacy, and Spiritual Care and Counseling.

Challenges

The outreach event did not go without some challenges. Those familiar with Nigeria weather will know that September falls into rainy season months, and as such, we were confronted with weather challenges. We experienced rain throughout the time we were in Ekiti for the mission. Thankfully, the rain came late evenings and at night. To avoid the types of weather problems that we experienced in 2018, we have decided to defer the medical mission for 2019. Our next Medical outreach is scheduled for February 2020 when rain is far less likely to be an issue.
