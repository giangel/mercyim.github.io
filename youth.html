YOUTH EMPOWERMENT

Positive Youth Development Programs

Mercy International Positive Youth Development Program is a broad based approach that aims to build the competencies, skills and abilities of youth that they need to grow and flourish throughout life. The approach flows from the principle of building mutually beneficial relationships between youth and their family, peer groups, school, workplace, community and culture to provide opportunities for youth to enhance their knowledge , interests, skills and abilities.

We do this by organizing training and conferences, creating opportunities for leadership development, training, mentorship and by increasing participants exposure to supportive and empowering environments where activities create multiple opportunities for a range of skill building and horizon broadening experiences.

Multiple factors influence how youth develop, thrive or struggle. Recognizing youth development as a function of and interaction between complex environments and systems can help us better respond to youth needs and development. Listed below are three core areas of youth development addresses by the Positive Youth Development Program. Leadership Development Mentoring, Counselling and Tutoring Facilitating Community Engagement and Support.

We continued to provide the necessary support to ten men and women in the local community who previously received empowerment loans to start or grow their small businesses. The aim of this program is to encourage self-help, put people’s destiny into their own hands and ultimately, to transform the communities. We maintain monthly follow-up meetings as a way of supporting their growth and address challenges facing them.

FOLLOWING ARE TESTIMONIALS FROM TWO OF THE BENEFICIARIES:

OLALEKAN TAIWO

I learned fashion designing but had no means of setting up. I depended on my parents for livelihood. The story soon changed when I became one of the beneficiaries of Mercy International mission’s empowerment project. With their financial assistance, I obtained a manual sewing machine to produce my fashions. Since that time, there has been a remarkable growth in the business. Now I have three manual sewing machines, four apprentices, and I am planning to buy an electrical sewing machine soon. I have also been able to become independent of my parents. May God bless Mercy’s mission and vision.

BUNMI ADEMILUA

I learned fashion designing several years back but had no funds to start a business. I started my business after I received funds from Mercy International. As a beneficiary of Mercy International mission’s empowerment scheme, I’ve added the sales of fabrics and sewing materials. I am now able to cater for myself, my parents, and siblings. Thank you Mercy International.
